# Dog Training Basics

Training your new dog builds trust and keeps them safe. Start with these fundamentals:

## Essential Commands
- **Sit**: Hold a treat above their nose, move it back. Mark and reward when they sit.
- **Stay**: Ask for sit, show palm, step back. Reward for staying in place.
- **Come**: Use an excited voice, reward generously when they come to you.
- **Leave it**: Cover a treat with your hand. Reward from the other hand when they back off.

## Training Principles
- Use positive reinforcement (treats, praise, play) — never punishment
- Keep sessions short (5-10 minutes) and fun
- Be consistent with commands and rules across all family members
- Train in low-distraction environments first, then gradually add challenges
- End on a success — even if you need to make it easy

## Socialization
- Expose your dog to different people, animals, sounds, and environments
- The critical socialization window for puppies is 3-14 weeks
- Always ensure positive experiences during socialization
- Puppy classes are great for socialization and basic obedience

## Common Issues
- Jumping: Ignore the behavior, reward four-on-the-floor
- Pulling on leash: Stop walking when they pull, resume when leash is slack
- Barking: Identify the trigger, redirect attention, reward quiet behavior
