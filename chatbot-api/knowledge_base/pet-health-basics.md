# Pet Health Basics

Keeping your pet healthy starts with preventive care. Here's what every pet owner should know:

## Veterinary Care
- Schedule annual check-ups (twice yearly for senior pets 7+)
- Keep vaccinations current (rabies, DHPP for dogs; FVRCP for cats)
- Discuss spay/neuter with your vet (recommended around 6 months)
- Dental cleanings as recommended by your vet

## Warning Signs to Watch For
- Sudden loss of appetite or weight loss
- Lethargy or unusual tiredness
- Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours
- Difficulty breathing or persistent coughing
- Lumps, bumps, or skin changes
- Changes in drinking or urination habits
- Limping or reluctance to move

## Preventive Care
- Monthly flea and tick prevention year-round
- Heartworm prevention (dogs especially)
- Microchip your pet for identification
- Keep toxic foods away: chocolate, grapes, onions, xylitol, garlic

## Emergency Contacts
- ASPCA Poison Control: (888) 426-4435
- Keep your vet's after-hours/emergency number saved
- Know the location of your nearest 24-hour emergency vet
